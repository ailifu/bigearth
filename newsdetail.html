Desert Locust Monitoring and Loss Assessment of Pakistan, India, Nepal

Author Source October 10, 2018

CASEarth working group “Monitoring and assessment of Desert Locust in Asia and Africa” has been conducting the research on dynamic monitoring of Desert Locust in Asian and African countries by combining multi-source Earth Observation data (including GF series in China, MODIS and Landsat series in the US, and Sentinel series in EU), meteorological data, field data, with independently developed models and algorithms. This report focuses on the latest development of Desert Locust monitoring and loss assessment in Pakistan, India, and Nepal.

The results show that, in July 2020, the desert locust harmed about another 628.3 thousand hectares of vegetation area in Pakistan, and about another 957.7 thousand hectares in India. The locust invaded Nepal on June 26, 2020, and harmed about 70.1 thousand hectares of vegetation area. At present, locusts in the summer breeding area on the Indo-Pakistan border are continuing multi-generation breeding. It is estimated that the first generation of summer locust swarms will be formed in mid-August and the second in September. The density of locusts in Nepal is relatively low and causes no major damages. The period from August to September coincides with the important crops growing season or harvesting season in Pakistan and India. If not properly controlled, locusts will bring a major threat to agricultural and pasture production of the two countries.

It is necessary to continue the monitoring and early warning of the intercontinental Desert Locust plague, and organize joint prevention and control in multiple countries, ensuring the safety of agricultural and pasture production, as well as regional stability.
